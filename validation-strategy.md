## Channel Details

### Tier 1: High Priority (Start Here)

#### 1. Direct Email to Local Tradespeople
**Why highest priority:** Personalized outreach has best conversion. Local tradespeople feel connection.

**Strategy:**
- Search Google Maps for "plumber [city]", "electrician [area]"
- Visit their websites for contact info
- Use cold email template (see below)
- Target: 20-30 emails, expect 3-5 signups

**Success Metric:** 15% open rate, 5% conversion to signup

#### 2. Facebook Groups (UK Trades)
**Best groups to target:**
- "UK Contractors" (~15k members)
- "Plumbers UK" (~8k members)
- "Electricians UK" (~12k members)
- "Handyman Services UK" (~6k members)
- "Construction UK" (~10k members)
- "Self-Employed Tradespeople UK" (~5k members)
- "UK Builders & Trades" (~7k members)

**Posting strategy:**
- Join 2-3 groups first, engage with other posts
- Wait 1-2 days before posting
- Use value-first approach (not pure promotion)
- See Facebook template below

**Success Metric:** 50 views, 3-5 signups per successful post

#### 3. LinkedIn Posts + Groups
**Groups to join:**
- "UK Contractors" (company page: UK Contractors Group)
- "IPSE - Self-Employed Professionals" (~50k members)
- "Self-Employed Tradespeople UK"
- "Construction Professionals UK"

**Posting strategy:**
- Post on personal profile first (see LinkedIn template)
- Cross-post to relevant groups
- Engage with comments within 2 hours
- Use hashtags: #UKContractors #Tradespeople #SmallBusiness #QuoteSoftware

**Success Metric:** 100 views, 5-10 signups

## Notes & Learnings

### Key Insights from Research

**From Indie Hackers validation guides:**
- Customer interviews are gold - but need access
- Pre-selling (collecting payments) is strongest validation
- 30 signups is reasonable threshold for MVP build
- Landing page + payment form is fastest validation loop

**From UK trades research:**
- Tradespeople value time over features
- SMS is preferred for quick communication
- Reviews and reputation drive decisions
- Facebook groups are active community hubs
- Screwfix forum has engaged tradespeople

**Common pain points mentioned:**
- "Quoting takes too long"
- "Customers don't respond to emails"
- "Paperwork eats into evening time"
- "Don't know if they've seen my quote"

### SaaS Validation Best Practices

1. **Customer interviews > surveys** (but need access first)
2. **Smoke test with landing page** (we have this âœ“)
3. **Pre-sell if possible** (we're offering discount, not pre-payment)
4. **Wait for strangers to sign up** (friends don't count)
5. **One stranger paying > 100 friends saying "cool idea"**
